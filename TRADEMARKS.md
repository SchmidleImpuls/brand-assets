# Trademark and Brand Usage Policy

Last updated: 2026-05-01

This policy governs use of the Schmidle Impuls, Steadlore, Steadlore Life, and Steadlore House names, logos, wordmarks, icons, visual identity, and other brand assets.

## 1. Owner

The Schmidle Impuls, Steadlore, Steadlore Life, and Steadlore House marks and associated brand assets are owned by Michael Schmidle, doing business as Schmidle Impuls, unless otherwise stated.

## 2. Open-source code is separate from brand rights

Some Schmidle Impuls software may be released under open-source licenses. Those licenses apply to the software code only.

They do not grant permission to use the Schmidle Impuls, Steadlore, Steadlore Life, Steadlore House names, logos, wordmarks, icons, or other brand assets.

## 3. Uses allowed without prior written permission

You may use the word marks in plain text to make truthful, non-misleading references, such as:

- “compatible with Steadlore”
- “uses Steadlore”
- “forked from Steadlore”
- “tutorial for Steadlore Life”
- “integration with Steadlore House”

These uses must not imply that your project, product, company, service, event, or content is official, endorsed, sponsored, certified, or affiliated with Michael Schmidle or Schmidle Impuls.

## 4. Uses requiring prior written permission

You need prior written permission to:

- use any logo, wordmark, icon, or visual brand asset;
- use a mark in a product, service, company, repository, package, app, domain, event, or merchandise name;
- create modified versions of the brand assets;
- use the marks in marketing, advertising, merchandise, or commercial promotion;
- imply certification, endorsement, sponsorship, partnership, or official status;
- register or attempt to register confusingly similar names, marks, domain names, package names, or repository names.

## 5. Forks and derivatives

Forks of open-source software must make clear that they are unofficial unless they are explicitly approved by Michael Schmidle / Schmidle Impuls.

Forks should use a distinct name and visual identity. They may describe their origin in plain text, for example:

> “This project is an unofficial fork of Steadlore.”

Forks must not use official logos or visual brand assets without prior written permission.

## 6. Proper use

When referring to the marks:

- use the mark as an adjective, not as a generic noun;
- preserve the spelling and capitalization;
- do not pluralize or abbreviate the marks;
- do not combine the marks with your own project or company name in a way that suggests official status.

Correct:

- “A plugin compatible with Steadlore”
- “An unofficial guide to Steadlore Life”

Incorrect:

- “SteadlorePro”
- “Steadlore by Example Ltd.”
- “Official Steadlore House Partner” without written permission

All rights not expressly granted are reserved.

Michael Schmidle / Schmidle Impuls may revoke permission for uses that violate this policy or create confusion, misuse, or reputational harm.
